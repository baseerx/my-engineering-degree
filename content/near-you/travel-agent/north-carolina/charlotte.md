## **How to Become a Travel Agent in Charlotte**

Like most states, North Carolina has no licensure requirement for travel agents. Individuals may secure placement with a travel agency and begin their careers.

However, candidates must have a high school diploma or GED to enroll in a training program. Those who have professional credentials are also typically favored. Luckily, there are a few options for Charlotte candidates looking to join professional organizations and become certified.

Among these are:

* American Society of Travel Advisors (ASTA),
* Travel Institute,
* Travel Industry Designator Service (TIDS) and the
* International Air Transport Association (IATA).

Most of these organizations will also expect candidates to renew their licenses by participating in training courses and passing periodic certification examinations.

### Professional Associations and Certifications

Candidates in Charlotte may achieve two key objectives simultaneously by joining a professional organization. This allows them to receive basic training while qualifying for professional credentials that bring numerous career opportunities.

For instance, associations like the American Society of Travel Advisors (ASTA) and the Travel Institute organize award-winning training programs, certification courses, and networking opportunities for travel agents.

After completing the basic training, candidates will immediately have a chance to keep improving by pursuing certifications such as Certified Travel Associate (CTA) or Certified Travel Counselor (CTC) from the same organizations.
