## **How to Become a Plumber in Detroit**

It is expected to see a plumber in Detroit, Michigan, start their careers by attending an academic program where they will earn either a diploma or a certificate. Of course, not all plumbers in the city start their careers this way.

Candidates may choose, for instance, to enroll in an apprenticeship program organized by trade unions or receive job training by working as an assistant to a master plumber in the city.

Working as a plumber in Detroit requires at least a journeyman license. After earning their journeyman license and working for two years, plumbers in the city may also pursue a master's claim and obtain a contractor license to start their own business and start taking on larger contracts.

## **Top Plumbing Schools in Detroit, Michigan**

The following are some of the top plumbing schools in and around Detroit, Michigan, for interested individuals to consider.

### **Macomb County Community College**

Macomb County Community College offers an apprenticeship program for aspiring plumbers in and around Detroit, Michigan. Candidates who enroll in this program will earn a general certificate and be able to specialize in any area of residential, commercial, and industrial plumbing.

As stated on the company website, candidates with an entrepreneurial spirit may even go on to start their businesses.

The program incorporates a lot of practical knowledge, including topics in fitting and valves, pipe calculations, valve installation and maintenance, and drainage flow, among many more.

### Detroit Training Institute of Technology

The Detroit Training Institute of Technology is another top-quality destination to receive training as an aspiring plumber in Detroit. The school is a green building trade school that was founded in 2006.

The program website describes that the plumbing courses are modern, incorporating both classroom and laboratory training.

The program is also very fitting for those looking for a fast-tracked destination to begin their careers. Students would have acquired enough skills in just four months to start a successful plumbing career.

The program also offers both a certificate and job assistance upon graduation.

### Randolph Career and Technical Center

At Rudolph Career and Technical Center, aspiring plumbers in Detroit may enroll in the Plumbing and Pipefitting program, where they will learn to assemble and construct various plumbing systems. They will also be able to repair and upgrade these systems as needed.

The focus topics include plumbing equipment maintenance, vent system installation, and hydronic heating systems installation.

The program includes classroom theoretical knowledge and laboratory practical skills.

### West Michigan Construction Institute

West Michigan Construction Institute has allowed students to pursue construction-related careers for a while now. The school offers a plumbing course covering critical concepts such as plumbing systems installation, testing, repair, and maintenance, along with safety principles for both residential and commercial usage.

Students will also learn about mathematics, schematics, and relevant codes.

Accepted applicants can complete their training in only a few months and start working in various plumbing establishments.

### Central Michigan University

Recognizing the nationwide shortage of skilled plumbers, Central Michigan University offers a **Foundations of Plumbing** training program teaching specialized plumbing skills and terminology in residential, commercial, and industrial systems.

The courses also teach mathematics, geometry, and physical principles of plumbing systems.

According to the program website, there are no prerequisites to take this course, and it is open to anyone interested in pursuing a plumbing career. The instruction materials are also included in enrollment and are made available online.

The program is offered with ed2go, so all courses are available online.

### Apprenticeship Programs

Candidates who would instead go for an apprenticeship program may enroll in the plumbing training programs offered by **Plumbers 98 and MCA Detroit**, with locations in Detroit, Madison Heights, and Troy.

Candidates may also consider the apprenticeship program offered by **UA Local Union 85**, based in Saginaw.
