## **How to Become an Automotive Mechanic in Detroit**

Becoming an automotive mechanic in Detroit starts with graduating from an accredited training program. The organization responsible for accrediting auto mechanic training programs is the National Automotive Technician Education Foundation (NATEF)

To gain responsibility for accrediting auto mechanic training programs admission into NATEF-accredited programs, candidates will most likely need a high-school diploma or GED.

After graduating from a training program, automotive mechanics with 2 years of work experience should apply for certification from the Automotive Service Excellence. In this regard, they have various alternative credentials to go for, including collision repair, damage analysis, medium-heavy trucks, and alternate fuels certification, among others.

Those working with refrigerants must also acquire an EPA 609 refrigerant certification.

## **Top Automotive Mechanic Schools in Detroit, Michigan**

The following are some of the most prominent training schools for aspiring auto mechanics in Detroit to consider.

### **Wayne County Community College**

Automotive technology students at Wayne County Community College participate in a degree program called the Automotive Service Technology program. As expected, the program prepares students for a career in the automotive industry by teaching them skills such as diagnosing, repairing, and servicing modern automobiles.

Candidates may choose between an associate of applied science degree and a college certificate. They will also be able to use this opportunity to prepare for the automotive service excellence certification exam and the State of Michigan certification for automotive professionals.

Wayne County Community College's automotive service technology program follows the NATEF standard.

### **Henry Ford Community College**

Henry Ford Community College is another top-class institution that provides automotive training to interested candidates in the Detroit area. The school offers students the chance to obtain the skills they will need to work as professional automotive mechanics and a unique chance to participate in the Ford Automotive Student Service Educational Training (ASSET) program.

Graduates would have received everything they need to kick-start a successful career through these opportunities. Training takes over two years, and individuals who complete their program will also leave with an associate degree.

### **Ferris State University**

[Ferris State University](https://www.ferris.edu/) in Big Rapids is a constant fixture among the top schools for quality automotive mechanic training in the state. Applicants of this program should be prepared to choose between a certificate and an associate degree program.

Regardless of their choice, they can secure entry-level positions in any establishment dealing with automotive services maintenance and repair.

### **Washtenaw Community College**

[Washtenaw Community College](https://www.wccnet.edu/) in Ann Arbor is popular for numerous trade programs, including the automotive technology program. Individuals who successfully enroll in this program can learn under impressive instructors who have worked in the field for numerous years.

Insight is an associate degree in automotive technology, and to acquire this degree, students will be put through rigorous classroom instructions and actual hands-on training. They will also get to obtain an Automotive Service Excellence certification of choice.

### **Muskegon Community College**

Muskegon Community College in Muskegon offers a comprehensive automotive technology program whose graduates will receive certificates and eligibility for other industry certification tests. Among these are the Automotive Service Excellence credentials that can help them specialize and stand out in front of potential employers.

The focus topics are steering and brake systems, automotive components, automatic transmissions, and collision repair.

The program can be completed in 12 months.
